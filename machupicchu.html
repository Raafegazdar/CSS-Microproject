Machu Picchu.

Machu Picchu, the ancient Incan citadel nestled in the Andes, is a breathtaking testament to Incan engineering and culture, often called the "Lost City of the Incas."

INTRODUCTION

Machu Picchu is a stunning 15th-century Incan citadel located high in the Andes Mountains of Peru. Renowned for its archaeological significance and breathtaking scenery, it embodies the ingenuity and artistry of the Inca civilization.Machu Picchu, an ancient Incan citadel built in the 15th century, is renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone construction and breathtaking mountain scenery. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it serves as a vital cultural symbol and a testament to the ingenuity of the Inca civilization.

Machu Picchu, often referred to as the "Lost City of the Incas," was built in the 15th century under the reign of Emperor Pachacuti. This UNESCO World Heritage site features remarkable stone structures, terraced fields, and sophisticated water management systems, all set against a backdrop of lush greenery and towering peaks, making it one of the most iconic symbols of Incan heritage and a must-visit destination for travelers.

Introduction

Machu Picchu is an iconic 15th-century Incan citadel set high in the Andes, renowned for its stunning architecture and breathtaking views..

Brief History

Machu Picchu was constructed in the mid-15th century under the Inca emperor Pachacuti and served as a royal estate or religious site. Rediscovered by American historian Hiram Bingham in 1911, it became a symbol of Inca civilization and is now a UNESCO World Heritage site

Important Details

Machu Picchu, located at 2,430 meters (7,970 feet) in the Andes Mountains of Peru, was constructed in the mid-15th century during the reign of Emperor Pachacuti. It is believed to have served as a royal estate, religious site, and agricultural center, showcasing advanced Incan engineering with its precise stonework and intricate layout.

The Timeline

Machu Picchu was built around 1450, abandoned during the Spanish conquest, rediscovered by Hiram Bingham in 1911, designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.

Building Cost

The exact cost of building Machu Picchu is unknown, but it involved extensive resources, labor, and time, reflecting the Inca civilization's remarkable engineering capabilities.

OVERVIEW

ADDRESS

Machu Picchu is located in the Cusco Region of Peru, specifically at the coordinates 13.1631° S latitude and 72.5449° W longitude.

CONSTRUCTION COST

The exact construction cost of Machu Picchu is unknown, but estimates suggest it would be equivalent to millions of dollars today due to the extensive labor and materials involved.

TIME TAKEN

Machu Picchu is believed to have been constructed over a period of about 20 years, primarily during the reign of Emperor Pachacuti in the mid-15th century.

SITUATED ON

Machu Picchu is situated on a ridge between the Huayna Picchu and Machu Picchu mountains, overlooking the Urubamba River in the Andes Mountains of Peru.

WORLD HERITAGE SITE

Machu Picchu was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 for its cultural and historical significance..

Did You Know?

Myths about the monument

Myths surrounding Machu Picchu include tales of it being the lost city of gold and a retreat for Inca royalty, as well as legends of its construction by giants.

A Marvel indeed

Machu Picchu features over 200 structures, including temples, terraces, and residences, built without mortar using precisely cut stones that fit together perfectly.

Sacrifices

Machu Picchu is thought to have been a site for religious ceremonies, including possible ritual sacrifices, as the Incas believed such offerings were essential to appease their gods and ensure agricultural fertility.
