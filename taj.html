TAJ MAHAL

Discover the epitome of love: the Taj Mahal, an architectural marvel and Mughal masterpiece showcasing unparalleled beauty.

INTRODUCTION

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is an architectural marvel and a symbol of eternal love. Commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632, it serves as a mausoleum for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The white marble structure is renowned for its intricate craftsmanship and stunning symmetry. Designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983, it attracts millions of visitors each year. The Taj Mahal's beauty and historical significance make it a true masterpiece of Mughal architecture. The Taj Mahal’s dome appears larger than the main mausoleum due to a clever optical illusion. Its size is intentionally designed to look proportionally larger as you approach it, creating a more impressive visual effect. Additionally, the Taj Mahal’s minarets are slightly tilted outward to create the illusion of straightness when viewed from a distance, ensuring that they appear perfectly vertical against the sky.

The Taj Mahal’s intricate marble inlay work features over 28 different types of gemstones, including jade, lapis lazuli, and agate, creating stunning floral patterns and geometric designs. The monument’s design incorporates elements of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architecture, blending these styles into a unique masterpiece. Its symmetrical layout is complemented by a precise reflection in the surrounding reflecting pool. The Taj Mahal’s white marble, known for its ability to change color with the light, enhances its ethereal beauty. Each corner of the monument has a minaret slightly tilted away from the main structure, ensuring they appear upright from afar and protecting the mausoleum in case of earthquakes.

Brief History

The Taj Mahal was commissioned in 1632 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It is an enduring symbol of love and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Important Details

The Taj Mahal is renowned for its stunning white marble architecture, intricate inlay work, and its symmetrical gardens. It attracts millions of visitors each year and is one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

The Timeline

The construction of the Taj Mahal took approximately 21 years. It began in 1632 and was completed in 1653. This timeline includes the construction of the main mausoleum, the surrounding gardens, and the various outbuildings.

Building Cost

The construction cost of the Taj Mahal was estimated to be around ₹32 million at the time it was built. In today's terms, this amount is roughly equivalent to $1 billion USD, considering inflation and changes in currency value.

Did You Know?

SECRET CHAMBERS

The Taj Mahal has hidden chambers below the main mausoleum, including a secret room that was believed to be intended for Shah Jahan himself.

ARCHITECTURAL PRECISION

The Taj Mahal's dome is perfectly symmetrical, with an intricate design that aligns precisely with the cardinal directions.

COLOR CHANGE

The color of the Taj Mahal changes throughout the day, reflecting shades of pink, orange, and white due to the varying light conditions.
